🇮🇳 The 5 Most Common Nutrient Deficiencies in India — What to Actually Prioritise

India has some of the highest rates of micronutrient deficiency globally. The ICMR-NIN National Nutrition Survey identifies five deficiencies affecting the majority of the Indian population: Vitamin D (70-80% of Indians are deficient due to limited sun exposure despite proximity to the equator, covered clothing, and melanin-related absorption reduction), Vitamin B12 (critically high in vegetarians — B12 is found almost exclusively in animal products), Iron (India has the world's highest anaemia burden — affecting 53% of women), Iodine (addressed partially by iodised salt), and Omega-3 fatty acids (predominantly plant-based Indian diets provide ALA but not EPA/DHA).

Our recommendation priority for Indian users: (1) Vitamin D3 2,000-5,000 IU daily, (2) Vitamin B12 500mcg methylcobalamin if vegetarian, (3) Omega-3 EPA+DHA if not eating fatty fish 2×/week, (4) Iron if clinically deficient (test first), (5) Multivitamin as insurance after the above.

🩸 Best Iron Supplements in India

India has the world's highest burden of iron deficiency anaemia — affecting 53% of women and 20% of men. Iron supplementation should only follow clinical blood test confirmation (serum ferritin, haemoglobin, TIBC). Do NOT self-supplement iron without testing — excess iron is toxic and causes GI damage. Get tested first, then supplement if confirmed deficient.

⚠️ Critical: Iron supplementation without confirmed deficiency can cause iron overload, oxidative stress, and gastrointestinal damage. Always get a serum ferritin test before starting iron supplementation. This is available at all major diagnostic labs in India for ₹300-600.

Frequently Asked Questions
Which vitamin is most commonly deficient in India?
Vitamin D is the most widespread deficiency — affecting an estimated 70-80% of Indians regardless of sun exposure levels. This is due to melanin in darker skin requiring 3-6× more sun exposure than lighter skin to produce equivalent Vitamin D, combined with indoor lifestyles, covered clothing, and dietary insufficiency. Vitamin B12 is second most critical for the large vegetarian population.
Do Indians need iron supplements?
Only if clinically confirmed deficient via a serum ferritin and haemoglobin blood test. India has the world's highest anaemia burden (53% of women), but iron supplementation without confirmed deficiency can cause iron overload — a serious condition causing liver damage and oxidative stress. Get a blood test first. Tests are available at all major diagnostic chains (Dr. Lal, SRL, Apollo) for ₹300-600.
Is it safe to take Vitamin D supplements long-term in India?
Yes — D3 (cholecalciferol) at 2,000-5,000 IU daily is safe for long-term use for most adults. Pair with Vitamin K2 (MK-7 form) to ensure calcium is directed to bones rather than arteries during extended D3 supplementation. Toxicity is only possible at sustained doses above 10,000 IU/day for months. Test serum 25(OH)D levels annually if supplementing long-term.
What is the best multivitamin for Indian athletes?
TrueBasics Advanced Multivitamin (men's or women's) for domestic option, or ON Opti-Men for an international option available on Amazon India. For athletes, supplement the multivitamin base with additional Vitamin D3 (5,000 IU) and Omega-3 (EPA+DHA) separately — no Indian multivitamin provides clinical doses of these critical nutrients in a single product.
Are Ayurvedic supplements as effective as conventional supplements?
For some specific applications, yes — Ashwagandha (anxiety/cortisol), Turmeric/Curcumin (anti-inflammatory), and Triphala (digestive health) have reasonable clinical evidence. For the core nutrient deficiencies (Vitamin D, B12, Iron, Omega-3), conventional micronutrient supplements are significantly more reliable — the dose and bioavailability of these nutrients in Ayurvedic herbal products are generally too low for clinical deficiency correction.
