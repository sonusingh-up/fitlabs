🇮🇳 Pre-Workout Reality in India — Import vs Domestic

Fully-dosed pre-workouts (6-8g citrulline, 3.2g beta-alanine, 300mg caffeine) are almost exclusively international brands — Transparent Labs, Legion, Gorilla Mode. These ship to India but cost ₹3,500-5,000/month after import duties. Domestic Indian pre-workouts (MuscleBlaze, Fast&Up, MuscleXP) are typically moderate-dose products with lower citrulline and caffeine doses suited to general gym use.

Regulatory note: India has no specific FSSAI category for pre-workout supplements. Most are sold under the "food supplement" classification. High-stimulant pre-workouts (350mg+ caffeine) face occasional customs scrutiny on import — stick to ≤250mg caffeine products for reliable customs clearance.

📖 India Pre-Workout Buyer's Guide

The One Number That Tells You If a Pre-Workout Actually Works: L-Citrulline Dose

L-citrulline is the pre-workout ingredient with the strongest evidence for improving exercise performance, blood flow, and pump — but it only works at 6-8g per dose. Almost every domestic Indian pre-workout (and several popular international brands like ON GS Pre) uses 750mg-2g citrulline — 10-25% of the effective dose. A pre-workout with 6g+ citrulline will produce a perceptibly different training experience from one with 750mg. This is the most important number to check on any pre-workout label.

Frequently Asked Questions
Which is the best pre-workout available on Amazon India?
ON Gold Standard Pre-Workout is the best combination of quality, availability, and reasonable pricing available on Amazon India. For more clinical dosing (especially citrulline), you need to import Transparent Labs BULK or Legion Pulse, which ship to India but add ₹500-1,500 shipping and potential customs fees.
Are pre-workouts legal in India?
Yes — pre-workout supplements are legal in India when sold as food supplements under FSSAI. There are no controlled substance restrictions on standard pre-workout ingredients (caffeine, citrulline, beta-alanine, creatine). Some extreme high-stimulant products containing DMAA or SARMs are restricted, but these are not standard gym pre-workouts from legitimate brands.
Why are Indian pre-workouts underdosed on citrulline?
Cost. L-citrulline at 6-8g per serving costs approximately ₹18-24 in ingredient cost alone. At ₹50/serving (standard Indian domestic pre-workout price), this leaves almost no margin for other ingredients or manufacturing. International brands charge ₹120-150/serving for fully-dosed products partly because of the higher ingredient cost for clinical citrulline dosing.
Is beta-alanine tingling harmful?
No — beta-alanine paresthesia (tingling sensation, particularly in the face, neck, and hands) is a harmless physiological response to the amino acid's interaction with sensory nerve receptors. It is temporary, fades within 30-60 minutes, and reduces with regular use. It is not a sign of toxicity or an adverse reaction. MuscleBlaze PRE (3g beta-alanine) will produce noticeable tingling — this is normal and indicates the dose is clinically relevant.
How much caffeine is too much in an Indian pre-workout context?
The EFSA safe upper limit is 400mg/day for healthy adults. Most moderate pre-workouts (175-200mg) leave room for a morning coffee. At 300mg+ pre-workout caffeine, avoid all other caffeine sources. Indian users should account for chai tea caffeine (25-40mg per cup) when calculating daily totals.
