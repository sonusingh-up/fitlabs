🇮🇳 India Market Reality — What You Need to Know

The Indian supplement market has specific challenges: adulteration is widespread (the FSSAI has flagged hundreds of products), prices vary enormously between platforms, and import duties make international brands 40–70% more expensive than US prices.

Our India recommendations prioritise: (1) FSSAI certification, (2) actual availability on Amazon India with genuine seller verification, (3) reasonable price per gram of active ingredient — not headline price. We do not recommend products sold only on brand websites without Amazon India presence, as these are harder to verify.

Frequently Asked Questions
Are supplements on Amazon India authentic?
Generally yes for established brands like MuscleBlaze, ON, and Dymatize when sold by the official brand store or verified sellers. Always check the seller name before purchasing. Avoid third-party sellers with no reviews selling branded products at suspiciously low prices.
What does FSSAI certification mean?
FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) certification means the product has been registered/approved for sale in India and meets basic food safety standards. It doesn't verify supplement efficacy, but it's the minimum quality assurance you should look for on any Indian supplement.
Should I buy international brands or Indian brands?
For creatine and basic whey: Indian brands (MuscleBlaze) offer excellent value and are FSSAI certified. For pre-workouts with clinical doses (8g citrulline, 300mg caffeine): international brands are better because Indian domestic options are typically underdosed. For NSF Certified products required for competition: import international.
