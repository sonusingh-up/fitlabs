The Bottom Line

Optimum Nutrition's Micronized Creatine is pharmaceutical-grade creatine monohydrate in four distinct delivery formats: the classic unflavoured Powder (5g/serving), convenient Capsules (2.5g per 2-capsule serving — requires double dosing for clinical effect), the new Gummies (5g total across 4 gummies, 1.25g each), and Creatine+ (5g creatine with additional electrolytes and vitamins). All four formats deliver the same molecule — creatine monohydrate — which has over 500 peer-reviewed studies and the ISSN's unconditional endorsement as the most effective ergogenic nutritional supplement for high-intensity exercise.

ON has been making pharmaceutical-grade creatine since the 1990s. Their quality controls, banned substance testing, and consistent dosing accuracy make this one of the most trusted creatine brands on the market — not just in sports nutrition generally, but specifically because ON is the official supplement partner of major professional sports leagues that drug-test their athletes.

The format you choose matters primarily for convenience and cost, not efficacy — the biological outcome of 5g creatine monohydrate per day is identical regardless of whether it arrives in a powder, a capsule, a gummy, or a functional drink mix. Choose the format that maximises daily adherence.

Full Verdict Breakdown

FORMULAScore: 10/10

Pure pharmaceutical-grade creatine monohydrate across all formats

All four ON creatine formats contain the same active compound: micronized creatine monohydrate. Micronized means the particle size has been reduced, improving solubility and reducing the slight grittiness common in non-micronized creatine. The purity is pharmaceutical-grade — ON manufactures in their own cGMP facilities with quality testing at multiple production stages.

The Creatine+ format adds a blend of electrolytes (sodium, potassium) and B vitamins alongside the 5g creatine — useful for athletes who also want a hydration supplement, though the electrolyte doses are modest (not equivalent to a dedicated hydration product). The core creatine content is identical across all formats.

DOSING VERDICTScore: 9.5/10

Powder, Gummies, and Creatine+ nail the 5g dose; Capsules require double the label serving

The Powder, Gummies (4 gummies), and Creatine+ all deliver the ISSN-recommended 5g daily maintenance dose at one serving. This is correct and convenient. The Capsule format provides 2.5g per 2-capsule serving — which means users need to take 4 capsules daily to achieve the clinical maintenance dose. This is an important distinction for capsule users: the serving size as labelled is half a clinical dose.

For loading phase users (optional): 20g/day in four divided doses for 5-7 days. Achievable with any format but most practical with the Powder mixed into multiple daily drinks.

VALUEScore: 9.2/10

Powder is exceptional value; Gummies are a convenience premium

The Powder at $0.38/serving is among the best value creatine monohydrate products available from a trusted brand with quality controls. BulkSupplements offers creatine at $0.10/serving, but without ON's quality infrastructure and banned substance testing. Thorne and Momentous offer NSF-certified creatine at $0.48 and $0.87 respectively — ON's Powder sits between these tiers in both price and certification level.

The Gummies at $1.33 per 5g serving are a meaningful convenience premium — appropriate for users who will not consistently take powder or capsules, and where improved adherence justifies the cost. Creatine you don't take is worthless; gummies you actually take every day beat powder you forget.

EVIDENCEScore: 10/10

The most evidence-backed ergogenic in all of sports nutrition — 500+ RCTs

Creatine monohydrate's evidence base requires no qualification. The ISSN Position Stand (Kreider et al., 2017) — based on systematic review of over 500 peer-reviewed studies across 30+ years — concludes creatine monohydrate is the most effective ergogenic nutritional supplement for increasing high-intensity exercise capacity and lean body mass during training. No other form of creatine (HCl, ethyl ester, buffered) has been shown to produce superior results in independent controlled human trials.

The specific evidence for ON's creatine products: ON's manufacturing quality and banned substance testing give confidence that the label dose is accurate and the product is free from contaminants. The performance evidence is for creatine monohydrate generally — the compound's pharmacology does not vary between legitimate pharmaceutical-grade manufacturers.

Phosphocreatine System Explained

Creatine is stored as phosphocreatine in skeletal muscle. During maximal-effort exercise (heavy lifting, sprinting), ATP is consumed faster than oxidative phosphorylation can replenish it. Phosphocreatine instantly donates its phosphate group to ADP via creatine kinase, regenerating ATP. Supplementing creatine monohydrate at 3-5g/day increases muscle PCr stores by 10-40%, extending the duration and power output in this fast energy system. The cumulative effect: more reps at a given weight, better sprint performance, faster recovery between sets, and greater long-term lean mass and strength gains from equivalent training.

Side Effects & Drug Interactions

⚠ Potential Side Effects
  • Intramuscular water retention in first week — normal and desired; makes muscles appear fuller
  • Minor weight gain (0.5-2kg) in first 2 weeks — water, not fat
  • Mild GI discomfort if entire loading dose taken at once — split doses fix this
  • No significant adverse effects documented in hundreds of safety studies at 3-5g/day
💊 Drug & Supplement Interactions
  • No significant drug interactions at standard maintenance doses
  • Kidney disease — standard precaution; creatinine elevation on blood tests is expected and not pathological
  • Avoid combining with diuretics or nephrotoxic medications during loading phases
