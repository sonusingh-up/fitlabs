What Is Dymatize ISO100, Really?

I'm going to tell you something that most protein powder reviews won't: ISO100 doesn't build more muscle than ON Gold Standard. If you're lactose-tolerant, training three or four days a week, and eating a reasonably high-protein diet, switching from Gold Standard to ISO100 will not move the needle on your body composition. I've tested both for extended periods. I know the research. The difference in muscle protein synthesis at matched doses is statistically negligible.

But here's what ISO100 does do, and does brilliantly: it works for people who can't comfortably tolerate standard whey. I developed mild lactose sensitivity at 31 — not dramatic, just enough bloating post-workout to make training feel punishing rather than rewarding. Within five days of switching to ISO100, it was gone. That's the actual use case for this product, and it's a legitimate one.

The formula itself is disarmingly simple: 100% hydrolysed whey protein isolate. Every trace of fat and lactose has been filtered out via cross-flow microfiltration. The remaining protein has been enzyme-treated to break the long polypeptide chains into smaller di- and tri-peptides, accelerating absorption. No WPC filler, no undisclosed blend ratios, no proprietary games. What you see is what you get.

📚 On absorption speed and muscle gains: Tang et al. (2009) in the Journal of Applied Physiology is the most-cited study on this. Hydrolysed whey does produce faster peak plasma amino acids (~40 min vs ~65 min for WPI). But at the 3-hour MPS measurement — the window that actually matters for muscle building — there was no statistically significant difference between hydrolysed WPI and regular WPI. The faster absorption matters for timing-sensitive athletes. For everyone else, it primarily affects digestive comfort.

What Hydrolysis Actually Does to Your Gut

Standard whey protein arrives in your stomach as intact polypeptide chains — long strings of amino acids that need your digestive enzymes to break them down before they can enter the bloodstream. Hydrolysis does that work in advance. The enzyme pre-treatment breaks the chains into di-peptides and tri-peptides small enough to use the gut's PEPT1 transporter — a separate, faster absorption pathway that doesn't require full enzymatic breakdown.

The practical benefit for gut tolerance is real. The smaller peptides create less digestive load, which is why people with lactose intolerance and some people with mild dairy sensitivity do better on hydrolysed WPI even after all the lactose has been removed. The smaller peptides simply ask less of your gut than intact proteins.

Who Actually Needs ISO100 (and Who Doesn't)

This is the conversation that matters. ISO100 costs roughly 30–90% more per serving than ON Gold Standard depending on which sizes you compare. That premium is only justified in specific situations.

✅ Buy ISO100 if you are:

  • Lactose intolerant — This is the primary reason this product exists. Zero lactose means zero bloating from the whey itself.
  • Training twice daily — If your recovery window between sessions is under 8 hours, the faster peak amino acid absorption has genuine value.
  • Tracking macros very tightly — 0.5g fat vs 1.5g, 2g carbs vs 3g. Not dramatic but meaningful across 60 servings.
  • Competing in drug-tested sport — Informed Choice certification covers this, same as Gold Standard.

❌ Stick with Gold Standard if you:

  • Are lactose-tolerant and training casually — You'll pay significantly more for no meaningful difference in muscle-building results. Save the money for food.
  • Avoid all artificial sweeteners — ISO100 uses sucralose with no unflavoured option in the standard range.
  • Need a vegan option — 100% dairy-derived. See our Best Protein Powders guide for plant alternatives.
