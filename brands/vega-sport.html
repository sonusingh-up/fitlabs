Brand Analysis

Vega Sport is the most widely available plant protein in mainstream retail — Target, Walmart, Whole Foods, and Amazon. NSF Certified for Sport on the Sport Premium Protein, which is the most important credential for plant protein athletes.

Sport Premium Protein delivers 30g protein and 2.6g leucine per scoop from pea, sunflower seed, and pumpkin seed — slightly less leucine than Garden of Life Sport (2.9g) but comparable across other metrics.

The wider retail availability is a genuine advantage over Garden of Life. If you need to buy plant protein in a physical store, Vega Sport is typically the best option. Online, Garden of Life Sport is marginally better on the leucine numbers.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Vega Sport a good protein?+

Yes for plant-based athletes. 30g protein + 2.6g leucine + NSF Certified. Good but slightly behind Garden of Life Sport on leucine (2.6g vs 2.9g).

Is Vega Sport NSF Certified?+

Yes — Sport Premium Protein carries NSF Certified for Sport certification. Tested for banned substances.

Vega Sport vs Garden of Life Sport?+

Both are 30g protein per scoop and NSF certified. Garden of Life has 2.9g leucine vs 2.6g and adds probiotics. Vega Sport is more widely available in stores. Garden of Life slightly better on nutrition profile.

Is Vega Sport good for vegans?+

Yes — fully vegan, plant-based ingredients, no animal products.

What does Vega Sport taste like?+

Better than most plant proteins. Still noticeably different from whey — slightly gritty, earthy undertone. Best mixed with a blender and fruit.
