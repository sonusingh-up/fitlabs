💪 Performance & Strength
🧬
Strength & Power
Creatine Monohydrate
The most studied performance supplement ever. Increases phosphocreatine stores by 20–40%, directly improving strength, power output, and muscle mass.
✔ Very Strong 3–5g/day
→
🩸
Pump & Blood Flow
L-Citrulline
Raises nitric oxide via the citrulline → arginine pathway. Clinical dose increases reps to failure by up to 52% in research. Most pre-workouts underdose it.
✔ Strong 6,000–8,000mg
→
⚡
Muscular Endurance
Beta-Alanine
Raises muscle carnosine — a pH buffer that delays the burn in high-rep sets and intervals. The tingling is harmless. Takes 4–8 weeks of daily use to fully load.
✔ Strong 3.2–6.4g/day
→
☕
Energy & Cognitive Performance
Caffeine
The most-studied ergogenic aid. Blocks adenosine receptors to reduce perceived effort and fatigue. Dose by bodyweight (3–6mg/kg), not by arbitrary mg count.
✔ Very Strong 3–6mg/kg BW
→
🏋️
Amino Acids
BCAAs (Leucine, Isoleucine, Valine)
Branched-chain amino acids including leucine — the primary trigger of muscle protein synthesis via mTORC1. Redundant if total daily protein is adequate.
~ Moderate 5–10g (3:1:1 ratio)
→
🛡️
Anti-Catabolic
HMB (β-Hydroxy β-Methylbutyrate)
A leucine metabolite that reduces muscle protein breakdown. Most useful during caloric deficits or detraining periods. Smaller benefit in trained individuals with adequate protein.
~ Moderate 3g/day
→
💎
Power Output
Betaine Anhydrous
Osmolyte that supports cellular hydration and reduces homocysteine. Studies show meaningful improvements in power output and body composition at 2,500mg. Often underdosed.
✔ Strong 2,500mg/day
→
🧠
Focus & Cognition
L-Tyrosine
Precursor to dopamine, norepinephrine, and thyroid hormones. Maintains cognitive performance under stress, sleep deprivation, and cold. Often found in pre-workouts at 500–1,000mg.
~ Moderate 500–2,000mg
→
💊 Protein Sources
💊
Complete Protein
Whey Protein
Highest leucine content of any protein source (~10%). Fastest digesting complete protein. Concentrate for value, isolate for lactose intolerance. 30+ years of evidence.
✔ Very Strong 20–40g per serving
→
🥛
Slow-Release Protein
Casein Protein
Slow-digesting protein (6–8 hours vs 1–2 for whey). Ideal before bed for overnight muscle protein synthesis. Complementary to whey in a complete protein strategy.
✔ Strong 25–40g before bed
→
🌱
Plant-Based Protein
Plant Protein (Pea, Rice, Soy)
Combined pea + rice protein achieves a complete amino acid profile comparable to whey. Lower leucine content — partially offset with larger servings. Best vegan alternative.
✔ Strong 25–40g per serving
→
🔬
Recovery & Gut Health
L-Glutamine
Most abundant amino acid in muscle and blood. Conditionally essential during intense training and illness. Strong evidence for gut health and immune function; weaker for muscle building.
~ Moderate 5–10g/day
→
🌙 Sleep & Recovery
🌙
Sleep Onset & Circadian
Melatonin
Circadian timing signal — not a sedative. 0.3–0.5mg is the research-backed dose. 95% of products are dosed 10–30× higher than necessary. Strong evidence for jet lag.
✔ Strong 0.3–0.5mg
→
🧲
Essential Mineral
Magnesium
~50% of western adults are deficient. Involved in 300+ reactions including sleep (GABA modulation), muscle relaxation, and ATP production. Form matters — avoid oxide (4% absorption).
✔ Strong 200–400mg elemental
→
🍃
Calm Focus & Sleep
L-Theanine
Promotes alpha brain waves — relaxed alertness without drowsiness. Pairs exceptionally with caffeine (reduces jitters). At 200mg before bed, reduces sleep latency and anxiety.
✔ Strong 100–200mg
→
💤
Sleep & Anxiety
GABA
The brain's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter. Oral GABA has poor blood-brain barrier penetration at standard doses, but novel delivery systems (nano-emulsion) show improved results.
~ Moderate 100–500mg
→
🌈
Serotonin Precursor
5-HTP
Converts directly to serotonin (and subsequently melatonin) in the brain. Evidence for sleep quality, mood, and appetite suppression. Caution with antidepressants (serotonin syndrome risk).
~ Moderate 50–200mg
→
🌼
Sleep & Anti-Anxiety
Apigenin
A chamomile flavonoid that binds GABA-A receptors with mild sedative-anxiolytic effects. Popularised by Andrew Huberman's sleep stack. 50mg before bed is the standard approach.
↑ Emerging 50mg before bed
→
🌿 Adaptogens & Hormonal Support
🌿
Adaptogen
Ashwagandha (KSM-66)
The adaptogen with the strongest evidence base. 27.9% cortisol reduction (double-blind RCT). Also shown to improve VO2 max, testosterone, sleep, and muscle recovery. Extract quality critical.
✔ Strong 300–600mg/day
→
🏔️
Adaptogen
Rhodiola Rosea
Scandinavian root adaptogen with solid evidence for reducing mental fatigue under stress and improving endurance exercise performance. Distinct mechanism from ashwagandha — can be combined.
✔ Moderate-Strong 200–600mg/day
→
⚙️
Essential Mineral
Zinc
Essential for testosterone production, immune function, and protein synthesis. Athletes commonly deficient due to sweat losses. Supplementation in deficient individuals reliably improves testosterone.
✔ Strong 15–30mg/day
→
🟤
Hormonal Support
Maca Root
Peruvian root with evidence for libido enhancement and mood improvement. Interestingly, appears to work without directly altering sex hormone levels — mechanism still being elucidated.
~ Moderate 1,500–3,000mg/day
→
☀️ Vitamins & Essential Nutrients
☀️
Essential Vitamin
Vitamin D3
~40% of the global population is deficient. Involved in testosterone synthesis, bone health, immune function, and mood. D3 (cholecalciferol) is the form that effectively raises blood levels.
✔ Very Strong 1,000–5,000 IU/day
→
🐟
Essential Fatty Acids
Omega-3 (EPA & DHA)
EPA and DHA are the active omega-3 forms from marine sources. Strong evidence for cardiovascular health, inflammation reduction, and joint health. Most people are chronically under-consuming.
✔ Very Strong 1,000–3,000mg EPA+DHA
→
🍊
Antioxidant & Immune
Vitamin C
Essential for collagen synthesis, immune function, and iron absorption. Strong evidence for reducing duration of colds in athletes under high training stress. Note: high-dose antioxidants may blunt some training adaptations.
✔ Very Strong 500–1,000mg/day
→
⚡
Mitochondrial Support
CoQ10 (Ubiquinol)
Essential component of the mitochondrial electron transport chain. Declines with age and statin use. Evidence for cardiovascular health, energy in older adults, and statin-induced myopathy relief.
✔ Strong 100–300mg/day
→
🔥 Weight Management & Thermogenics
🍵
Thermogenic
Green Tea Extract (EGCG)
EGCG catechins inhibit catechol-O-methyltransferase (COMT), extending norepinephrine signalling and increasing fat oxidation. Most effective when paired with caffeine. Clinical dose: 400–500mg EGCG.
✔ Strong 400–500mg EGCG
→
🧸
Appetite Suppression
Glucomannan
Soluble dietary fibre from konjac root. Absorbs water and expands in the stomach — mechanically increasing satiety. One of the few non-stimulant weight loss ingredients with FDA-reviewed evidence.
✔ Strong 3g/day (before meals)
→
🌶️
Thermogenic
Cayenne Pepper (Capsaicin)
Capsaicin activates TRPV1 receptors, triggering thermogenesis and temporarily increasing metabolic rate by 4–5%. Consistent daily use shows modest but real reductions in ad libitum calorie intake.
~ Moderate 2–6mg capsaicin/day
→
☕
Glucose Metabolism
Green Coffee Bean Extract
Chlorogenic acids (the active compounds) inhibit glucose-6-phosphatase, reducing hepatic glucose output and post-meal blood glucose spikes. Evidence for modest weight loss — not primarily via thermogenesis.
~ Moderate 400–800mg/day
→
