What Is Casein?

Casein makes up approximately 80% of the protein in cow's milk — whey constitutes the remaining 20%. Unlike the globular whey proteins that dissolve readily in acidic conditions, casein forms a gel or clot in the acidic environment of the stomach. This coagulation dramatically slows gastric emptying and subsequent digestion, producing a slow, sustained release of amino acids into the bloodstream over 6–8 hours.

Evidence for Overnight Use

📊 Res et al. (2012) — Landmark Overnight MPS Study
40g casein protein consumed 30 min before sleep significantly increased overnight MPS (+22%) vs placebo and maintained positive whole-body protein balance throughout the night.
This is the foundational study establishing pre-sleep casein as an effective strategy for muscle building. Participants were resistance-trained males who had completed a late-evening training session.
📊 Snijders et al. (2015) — 12-Week Training RCT
Nightly pre-sleep casein supplementation (28g) combined with resistance training produced significantly greater increases in muscle mass and strength vs placebo over 12 weeks.
This established that the acute overnight MPS effect from Res et al. translates to chronic muscle accretion over weeks and months of training — the practical question that matters for athletes.

Dosage

25–40g of casein protein taken 30–60 minutes before sleep. The dose used in the Res et al. study was 40g — sufficient to maintain positive protein balance throughout an 8-hour sleep period. Smaller doses (25g) still provide meaningful benefit but may not sustain amino acid elevation as long.

Who Benefits

  • Hard training athletes with high protein targets — pre-sleep casein is an efficient way to increase daily protein without an extra meal
  • Those who cannot eat frequently during the day — casein's sustained release makes it the best option for long fasted periods
  • Older adults (50+) — evidence suggests pre-sleep protein is particularly valuable for combating age-related overnight muscle protein breakdown
  • Caloric restriction/fat loss — high satiety per calorie; thick consistency reduces hunger overnight

Timing Strategy — Using Both Casein and Whey

The optimal protein strategy uses both whey and casein at different times:

  • Post-workout: Whey protein — fast-acting, high leucine, maximises the acute MPS response when muscles are most receptive
  • Before bed: Casein — slow-digesting, maintains amino acid availability throughout the sleep period when overnight MPS and recovery occur
  • Between meals (>4 hours): Casein or a casein/whey blend — sustained amino acid availability reduces muscle protein breakdown during extended fasting periods
